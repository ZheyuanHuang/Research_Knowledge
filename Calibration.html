1. Persuasive Calibration
Yiding Feng & Wei Tang
SODA 2026 (Forthcoming) | Economics of AI & Decision-Making
Focus: Theoretical characterization of optimal predictor design under calibration error (ECE) constraints and incentive misalignment. Explicitly frames predictor design as information design using mean-preserving contractions (MPC).
Key Innovation: Two-step view decomposing calibrated predictor into (1) perfectly calibrated predictor (signaling scheme) and (2) miscalibration plan. LP-based algorithms for computation.

2. Costly Miscalibration
Yingni Guo & Aran Shmaya
Theoretical Economics 16(2), 2023 | 62 citations
Focus: Sender-receiver game where platform provides probabilistic forecasts. Introduces concept of miscalibration cost, bridging cheap-talk and persuasion models. Explicitly frames calibration as information design problem.
Key Finding: Miscalibration cost intensity proxies platform's commitment power. Shows how different cost levels affect equilibrium forecasts and customer responsiveness.

3. Calibrated Forecasting and Persuasion
Atulya Jain & Vianney Perchet
EC 2024 | New Haven, CT
Focus: Dynamic game where expert sends calibrated probabilistic forecasts. Reduces dynamic game to static Bayesian persuasion problem. Characterizes implementable forecast distributions as mean-preserving contractions.
Key Result: Distribution of forecasts implementable by calibrated strategy iff it's mean-preserving contraction of honest forecasts. Connects dynamic calibration to static information design.

4. Forecasting for Swap Regret for All Downstream Agents
Aaron Roth & Mirah Shi
EC 2024 | New Haven, CT | 29 citations
Focus: Theoretical characterization of calibration's role in guaranteeing no-swap-regret for ALL downstream decision-makers with arbitrary utility functions. Core information design problem: predictor must inform multiple agents simultaneously.
Key Result: Non-calibrated but unbiased predictions improve regret bounds vs. fully calibrated forecasts. Achieves O(‚àöT) rate in 1D and O(T^2/3) dimension-independent rate in high dimensions.

5. Forecast Hedging and Calibration
Dean P. Foster & Sergiu Hart
Journal of Political Economy 129(12), 2021 | 35 citations
Focus: Theoretical framework connecting calibration to revelation principle and economic equilibrium. Develops forecast hedging concept: choosing forecasts to guarantee expected track record only improves.
Key Contribution: Unifies calibration results via simple proof scheme. Shows connection between calibration and correlated equilibrium. Provides new calibrated forecasting procedure for binary events.

üí° Key Insights & Themes

Information Design Connection: Primary papers reframe calibration as information design‚Äîsignals (predicted probabilities) are constrained to match posterior means. Tools from Bayesian persuasion characterize optimal design.
Mean-Preserving Contractions: Implementable forecast distributions must be mean-preserving contractions of honest forecasts. This constraint appears across multiple papers as core analytical tool.
Decision-Maker Guarantees: Calibration emerges as bridge between forecasting and downstream decision-maker welfare. Multiple papers show how calibration ensures no-regret, no-swap-regret properties for agents.
Commitment & Credibility: Calibration cost/constraint acts as commitment device, giving predictor credibility in sender-receiver games. This connects reputation concerns to information structure design.
Platform Design Applications: Primary motivation across papers includes CTR prediction in auctions, credit scoring, criminal justice algorithms, healthcare decision support‚Äîall domains where information provision affects downstream choices.
