What is WINE?

The Conference on Web and Internet Economics (WINE) is a premier international forum dedicated to exploring the intersection of algorithmic theory, computational complexity, and economic principles in the context of the Internet and the World Wide Web.

Originally called the Workshop on Internet and Network Economics (until 2013), WINE has evolved into a leading platform where researchers from diverse disciplines converge to address fundamental questions about incentives, computation, and market design in digital ecosystems.

Why WINE Matters

The Internet has fundamentally transformed economics. Problems such as resource allocation, mechanism design, and market equilibrium now occur at unprecedented scale with millions of autonomous agents. WINE addresses these challenges by combining:

  • Algorithmic Analysis: Understanding computational complexity of economic problems
  • Game Theory: Modeling strategic behavior in decentralized systems
  • Mechanism Design: Creating incentive-compatible protocols and markets
  • Empirical Study: Analyzing real-world Internet and platform ecosystems

Publication & Recognition

WINE proceedings are published by Springer-Verlag, one of the world's leading academic publishers. Selected papers are invited for extended publication in ACM Transactions on Economics and Computation (TEAC), a top-tier venue for peer-reviewed research in algorithmic game theory and computational economics.

Real-World Applications

WINE research directly applies to pressing problems in modern digital economies:

  • Search & Advertising Markets: How search engines allocate ad slots fairly and efficiently
  • Ride-Sharing Platforms: Dynamic pricing and matching algorithms (Uber, Lyft)
  • E-Commerce: Recommendation systems and seller-buyer matching (Amazon, eBay)
  • Blockchain & Web3: Incentive mechanisms for decentralized networks
  • Social Networks: Information flow, viral marketing, and network effects
  • Cloud Computing: Resource allocation and pricing in IaaS/PaaS markets

20-Year History & Milestones

1

2005: Workshop Origins

WINE begins as the "Workshop on Internet and Network Economics" with the inaugural gathering of researchers interested in economic aspects of the Internet.

2

2010s: Explosive Growth

The workshop matures into a full conference. Research expands to include blockchain, crowdsourcing, and platform economics. Proceedings published by Springer-Verlag.

3

2013: Rebranding to WINE

Renamed to "Conference on Web and Internet Economics" reflecting its expanded scope and maturity as a premier venue for the interdisciplinary community.

4

2015-2019: Global Reach

WINE conferences held in leading research centers worldwide: New York, Bangalore, Oxford, and more. Community grows with 300+ researchers annually.

5

2020: Virtual Expansion

WINE 2020 (held at Peking University) reaches record 300+ attendees. First major virtual conference, enabling global participation. ACM TEAC special issues established.

6

2021-2025: Continued Excellence

Recent conferences in Potsdam, Troy, Shanghai, and Edinburgh showcase cutting-edge research in emerging areas: Web3, AI economics, fairness in algorithms, and pandemic resource allocation.
