What is ACM EC?

The ACM Conference on Economics and Computation (EC) is the leading scientific conference on advances in theory, empirics, and applications at the interface of economics and computation. Since its inception in 1999, EC has been sponsored by the ACM Special Interest Group on Economics and Computation (SIGecom).

The conference brings together researchers and practitioners who are revolutionizing how we understand markets, design systems, and solve real-world economic challenges using computational thinking.

üéØ Core Mission

To incorporate theories of economic and strategic interaction into the design of computational systems that provide platforms for human and commercial interaction on the Internet. EC demonstrates how computational models improve our economic understanding of Internet-based markets and social networks.

Key Focus Areas

  • Theory: Algorithmic game theory, mechanism design, and market equilibrium
  • Empirics: Lab experiments, field experiments, and data-driven analysis
  • Applications: Real-world implementations in e-commerce, digital platforms, and online markets

üìñ Conference History & Evolution

1999 - The Beginning

The first ACM Conference on Electronic Commerce was established, laying the foundation for a new field combining economics and computation.

2000s - Growth Phase

EC grew into the premier international venue. EC became the go-to conference for algorithm design, auction theory, and market mechanisms research.

2010s - Diversification

The conference expanded to cover blockchain, cryptocurrencies, AI-driven markets, and platform economics. International locations including Europe and beyond.

2020s - Cutting Edge

Focus on AI ethics, fairness, privacy, and economic implications of large language models. Integration of empirical work with theoretical advances.

2025 - EC'25 at Stanford

The 26th edition takes place at Stanford University (July 7-10, 2025), featuring the latest advances in economics and computation.

How to Participate

1. Submit a Paper

Researchers can submit original research contributions addressing any of the conference topics. Papers go through peer review and must follow ACM formatting guidelines.

2. Attend as Participant

Researchers, students, and industry professionals can attend to learn about cutting-edge research, network with peers, and engage in discussions.

3. Organize Workshops

Submit proposals for workshops or tutorials on specialized topics to deepen knowledge in specific areas of economics and computation.

4. Present a Poster

Showcase work-in-progress or additional research through poster presentations to facilitate interactions and feedback.
