Overview

The International Conference on Information Systems (ICIS) is the most prestigious and exclusive annual gathering of academics and research-oriented practitioners in the field of information systems worldwide. Hosted by the Association for Information Systems (AIS), an international professional organization, ICIS stands as one of the most selective and high-impact conferences in the entire field.

Key Identity: ICIS is recognized as the flagship conference of the Association for Information Systems and attracts the top research papers in the information systems field, along with their authors, from more than 95 countries for invaluable networking and scholarly exchange.

Conference Characteristics

  • Annual Event: Held every year, typically in early December
  • Duration: Four-day conference format
  • Global Participation: Attracts scholars and practitioners from over 95 countries
  • Selective Venue: Highly exclusive with rigorous peer-review process
  • Research Focus: Emphasizes original, high-quality research in information systems

Historical Background

Foundation and Early Years

ICIS was founded in 1980 at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). The first conference was held in Philadelphia in the same year, marking the beginning of a legacy that would establish it as the premier venue for information systems research. Originally known as the Conference on Information Systems (CIS), the conference later expanded its international reach and adopted the name International Conference on Information Systems.

International Expansion

A significant milestone occurred in 1990 when ICIS held its first international venue in Copenhagen, Denmark. This expansion marked a strategic shift toward becoming a truly global conference. Since then, ICIS has been intentionally rotated across different geographic regions to promote international participation and diverse scholarly perspectives.

Regional Rotation System

To ensure truly global representation and accessibility, ICIS currently rotates its annual conference among three major AIS regions:

  • Region 1 (Americas): North America and South America
  • Region 2 (Europe/Middle East/Africa): Europe, Middle East, and Africa
  • Region 3 (Asia/Australia/Pacific): Asia, Australia, and Pacific regions

This rotation system ensures that the conference remains geographically balanced and accessible to researchers and practitioners from all parts of the world.

ICIS Doctoral Consortium

The ICIS Doctoral Consortium is a premier development opportunity held in conjunction with the main conference, typically at a nearby location before the main conference begins. This selective program brings together:

  • Senior faculty members with extensive experience in information systems research
  • Approximately 40 doctoral students, carefully selected from around 120 nominations
  • Each university or institution may nominate only one doctoral student participant

The consortium provides doctoral students with mentorship, feedback on their dissertation research, and valuable networking opportunities with leading scholars in the field. It has become a crucial launching point for many information systems scholars' careers.

The Association for Information Systems (AIS)

ICIS is hosted and organized by the Association for Information Systems (AIS), an international professional organization dedicated to advancing information systems as a discipline and profession. AIS is committed to:

Global Community Building

Fostering a worldwide community of information systems scholars, researchers, and practitioners committed to advancing the field.

Research Excellence

Promoting rigorous, high-quality research that advances theoretical understanding and practical application in information systems.

Knowledge Dissemination

Facilitating the sharing of research findings, best practices, and innovations through its flagship conferences and publications.

Professional Development

Supporting the career development of IS scholars at all stages, from doctoral students to established researchers.

Areas of Research & Tracks

ICIS encompasses a comprehensive range of information systems research areas. With 25+ dedicated tracks, the conference covers:

  • IS Foundations: Theory, philosophy, and conceptual frameworks in IS
  • IT Infrastructure & Architecture: Systems design, cloud computing, and IT governance
  • Data Science & Analytics: Big data, business intelligence, and advanced analytics
  • Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning: AI applications, automation, and intelligent systems
  • Cybersecurity & Privacy: Security management, data protection, and risk
  • Digital Business & E-Commerce: Digital transformation, digital entrepreneurship, and online business
  • Organizational Issues: IS management, organizational change, and IS strategy
  • IS in Society: Social implications, digital divide, and ICT4D (Information and Communication Technology for Development)
  • Global Information Systems: International IS issues, cultural dimensions, and global collaboration
  • Healthcare IT: Health information systems and digital health solutions
  • IT Project Management: Agile methods, software development practices, and project success
  • Human-Computer Interaction: User experience, usability, and interaction design

Significance & Academic Impact

ICIS holds tremendous significance within the academic and professional information systems community:

Prestigious Venue: Publication at ICIS is considered a major achievement for IS researchers and significantly impacts academic careers, promotions, and funding prospects.

Why ICIS Matters

  • Quality of Research: The rigorous peer-review process ensures only the highest quality research is presented
  • Exclusive Selection: High rejection rate (typically around 20-25% acceptance) ensures only the best work is presented
  • Career Advancement: Presenting at ICIS significantly enhances researchers' professional visibility and credibility
  • Networking Opportunities: The conference brings together the world's leading IS researchers and practitioners for collaboration
  • Knowledge Advancement: ICIS drives the evolution of information systems thinking and practice
  • Industry-Academia Connection: The conference bridges the gap between academic research and industry applications
  • Diversity of Perspectives: Global participation ensures diverse methodological approaches and cultural viewpoints

Key Features of ICIS

Rigorous Review Process

Double-blind peer review ensures objective evaluation of all submissions and maintains the highest standards of research quality.

Diverse Research Formats

Accepts completed research papers, short papers, panels, workshops, and other innovative formats for knowledge sharing.

Multi-Disciplinary Approach

Brings together perspectives from computer science, business, social sciences, and other disciplines to enrich IS research.

Networking Platform

Provides extensive opportunities for researchers to network, collaborate, and build lasting professional relationships.

Global Accessibility

Through regional rotation and diverse representation, ICIS maintains accessibility for researchers worldwide.

Open Access Repository

AIS eLibrary provides free access to decades of ICIS research papers, promoting open science and knowledge sharing.
